Q1: The Number Machine

A machine takes a number, multiplies it by 3, then subtracts 5.

If the machine outputs 19, what number was put in?

Step-by-step:

Work backwards: 19 + 5 = 24, then 24 ÷ 3 = 8.
Check: 8 × 3 = 24, then 24 − 5 = 19 ✔

Answer: 8

Number Theory ★ Easy
Q2: The Coin Jar

Riyansh has a jar of coins. Counting in groups of 4, he has 3 left over. Counting in groups of 5, he has 2 left over.

If the jar has fewer than 30 coins, how many coins are there?

(Hint: Try numbers that give remainder 3 when ÷ 4, then check which also give remainder 2 when ÷ 5.)

Step-by-step:

Numbers with remainder 3 when ÷ 4: 3, 7, 11, 15, 19, 23, 27
Check remainder when ÷ 5:
• 7 ÷ 5 = 1 r 2 ✔
• 27 ÷ 5 = 5 r 2 ✔
Both 7 and 27 work!

Answer: 7 or 27

Geometry ★★ Medium
Q3: The Garden Path

A rectangular garden is 12 m long and 5 m wide. A path of width 1 m runs around the outside.

What is the area of the path (in m²)?

(Hint: Think about the bigger rectangle that includes the path.)

Step-by-step:

Outer rectangle: (12+2) × (5+2) = 14 × 7 = 98 m²
Inner garden: 12 × 5 = 60 m²
Path area: 98 − 60 = 38 m²

Answer: 38

Combinatorics ★★ Medium
Q4: The Handshake Problem

There are 6 people at a party. If every person shakes hands with every other person exactly once, how many handshakes happen in total?

Step-by-step:

Person 1 shakes with 5, Person 2 with 4 new, Person 3 with 3 new...
Total: 5 + 4 + 3 + 2 + 1 = 15
Formula: n(n−1)/2 = 6×5/2 = 15

Answer: 15

Logic ★★★ Hard
Q5: The Treasure Chest Code

A treasure chest has a 3-digit code lock. Clues:

• All three digits add up to 12
• The hundreds digit is double the ones digit
• The tens digit is 4 more than the ones digit

What is the 3-digit code?

(Hint: Let the ones digit = some number, then figure out the others.)

Step-by-step:

Let ones = x. Then hundreds = 2x, tens = x + 4.
2x + (x+4) + x = 12 → 4x + 4 = 12 → x = 2
Ones = 2, Hundreds = 4, Tens = 6 → Code = 462
Check: 4+6+2 = 12 ✔ | 4 = 2×2 ✔ | 6 = 2+4 ✔

Answer: 462
